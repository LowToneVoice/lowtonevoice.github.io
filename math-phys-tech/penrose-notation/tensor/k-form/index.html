k-form and operations

k-form

Basis of k-form is \[ dx^{\mu_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge dx^{\mu_k} = \sum_{P\in\mathfrak{S}} \mathrm{sgn}(P) dx^{\mu_{P(1)}}\otimes\cdots \otimes dx^{\mu_{P(k)}} = \epsilon_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'}^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} dx^{\mu_1'}\otimes\cdots \otimes dx^{\mu_k'} \] so that \[ \omega = \omega_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} dx^{\mu_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge dx^{\mu_k} = \omega_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} \epsilon_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'}^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} dx^{\mu_1'}\otimes\cdots \otimes dx^{\mu_k'} . \]

We can write k-form with Levi-Civita symbol as the follows:

A thick line represents Levi-Civita symbol. The rectangle labeled \(\omega\) means \(\omega_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'}\). This entire symbol reflects \(\omega_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'}\epsilon_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k}^{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'}\).

We do not write symbols of basis \(dx\), since it is difficult to represent the basis. We interpret an up-open branch is connected to basis \(\partial_i\) and down-open branch to \(dx^\mu\).

Lie derivative

Wedge product

Wedge product satisfies \[ \begin{array}{ll} & ( \xi_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} dx^{\mu_1} \wedge\cdots\wedge dx^{\mu_k} ) \wedge ( \eta_{\mu_{k+1}\cdots\mu_{k+l}} dx^{\mu_{k+1}} \wedge\cdots\wedge dx^{\mu_{k+l}} ) \\ &= \xi_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} \eta_{\mu_{k+1}\cdots\mu_{k+l}} \epsilon^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k\mu_{k+1}\cdots\mu_{k+l}}_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'\mu_{k+1}'\cdots\mu_{k+l}'} dx^{\mu_1'} \otimes\cdots\otimes dx^{\mu_{k+l}'} . \end{array} \] This is represented in Penrose's graphical notation by connecting thick lines of Levi-Civita symbols.

Exterior product

Interior product

Interior product is computed as \[ \iota_V ( \omega_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} dx^{\mu_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge dx^{\mu_k} ) = \omega_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k} \epsilon^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_k}_{\mu_1'\cdots\mu_k'} V^{\mu_1'}dx^{\mu_2'} \otimes\cdots\otimes dx^{\mu_k'} . \] In graphical notation, the branch at the beginning is connected to \(V\).

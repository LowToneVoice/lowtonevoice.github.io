Fundamentals

In Penrose graphical notation, a tensor is represented as a box with as many sticks as its order. It is possible to distinguish between covariance and contravariance. The downward stick represents covariance, and upward contravariance.

Kronecker delta is represented as a stick.

A horizontal thick stick represents antisymmetry. Odd substitution of the position of the vertical stick reverses the sign. In this notation, Levi-Civita symbol is displayed as below:

The product of covariant and contravariant Levi-Civita symbols \(\epsilon^{ij\cdots k}\epsilon_{pq\cdots r}\) is drawn as the below, according to Penrose's article.

3-dim

Contraction in general case

\begin{align} \epsilon^{ijk}\epsilon_{pqr} = \left| \begin{matrix} \delta^i_p & \delta^i_q & \delta^i_r \\ \delta^j_p & \delta^j_q & \delta^j_r \\ \delta^k_p & \delta^k_q & \delta^k_r \end{matrix} \right| \end{align}

This formula is represented with Kronecker delta as the belows:

Connect top three points and bottom without leaks and doubles. That is, the number of terms is permutation 3!. The signing can be determined to some extent by "how many intersections there are". Nevertheless, as shown in the figure below, this rule can be broken if the line is written in a circuitous manner, so it is safe to consider "how many substitutions will return to the even-substitution form."

Contraction formulas

When using Levi-Civita symbol, some indexes are usually the same; connect the same indexes with Kronecker delta.

In case two or more kinds of indexes are the same, you have to consider permutation, because the same indexes are reversible.

If all indexes are the same, \(\epsilon^{ijk}\epsilon_{ijk}=3!\).

4-dim

As well as 3-dimensional Levi-Civita symbol, though Minkovski metric changes some signs.

Contraction in general case

\begin{align} \epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{pqrs} = \left| \begin{matrix} \delta^i_p & \delta^i_q & \delta^i_r & \delta^i_s \\ \delta^j_p & \delta^j_q & \delta^j_r & \delta^j_s \\ \delta^k_p & \delta^k_q & \delta^k_r & \delta^k_s \\ \delta^l_p & \delta^l_q & \delta^l_r & \delta^l_s \end{matrix} \right| \end{align}

Contraction formulas

You can calculate contraction formulas as of 3-dim.

It is wiser to leave Levi-Civita symbols than to calculate it. The permutation follows as well as 3-dim.

If all indexes are the same, \(\epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{ijkl}=4!=24\).

Minkovski metric

The above argument only holds in Euclid metric, \(g_{ij}=g^{ij}=\delta_{ij}\). Since the three spacial or one time component of metric tensor is negative in Minkovski metric, covariant and contravariant tensor has opposite signs.

\begin{align} \epsilon^{ijkl}=g^i_pg^j_qg^k_rg^l_s\epsilon_{pqrs}=-\epsilon_{pqrs} \qquad (\{i,j,k,l\}=\{p,q,r,s\}=\{0,1,2,3\}) \end{align}

Therefore, the signs of all the result above is reversed. That is,

\begin{align} \begin{array}{rcl} \epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{pqrl} &=& - \left| \begin{matrix} \delta^i_p & \delta^i_q & \delta^i_r \\ \delta^j_p & \delta^j_q & \delta^j_r \\ \delta^k_p & \Delta^k_q & \delta^k_r \end{matrix} \right|, \\ \epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{pqkl} &=& -2 \left| \begin{matrix} \delta^i_p & \delta^i_q \\ \delta^j_p & \delta^j_q \end{matrix} \right|, \\ \epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{pjkl} &=& -6\delta^i_p, \\ \epsilon^{ijkl}\epsilon_{ijkl} &=& -24. \end{array} \end{align}

This is what is written on The classical Theory of Fields by L. D. Landau and E. M. Lifshitz.
